The Hardship

Long story short: Living Gluten-Free sucks.

What is Celiac disease?

Celiac disease is a life long condition where the body thinks gluten is a threat. The only cure known to science at this moment in time is a life long commitment to the gluten free diet. Continued comsumption of gluten with Celiac disease causes permanent damage to the intestines that leads to malabsorption, joint pain, osteoporosis, a decrease in life expectancy, and more.

How I discovered I was Celiac

I have been lactose intolerant since around 16 years old. Around two years ago, I had unexplained diarrhea. My assumption was that it was because I was consuming too much milk products. Every time I would have an upset stomach I would avoid eating dishes that contained cheese like pasta and hamburgers. I would then eat dishes such as fried rice or some other type of rice dish that does not have milk products. Then, I discovered I would still get an upset stomach even when eliminating milk products from my diet. I suspected that it could be that I was Celiac(allergic to gluten). I went to the doctor and they confirmed I indeed have Celiac disease.

How I Feel Being Celiac

Living gluten free has been very difficult. I unable to consume my favourite foods like fried chicken and pasta. In addition, gluten is present in A LOT of grocery items that are naturally due to cross contamination. For example, soybeans the main ingredient for tofu is grown in rotation with wheat. The process of processing and transporting the soybeans without being cross-contaminated with gluten is very diffucult. That is why if you go to a grocery store, most if not all tofu's are not "certified gluten-free." Yes, there is a difference between "gluten-free" and "certified gluten-free." I have learned that my body has become more sensitive to gluten over time. I have found eating foods that do not permanently damage my body is impossible unless I eat plain whole foods such as vegtables, rice, and meat. The feeling of not being able to eat what other people enjoy on a daily basis is saddening.
